The plan was to be there by Sunday and leave the camera in its bag until Tuesday. The plan held until Monday at six, when the side street I was crossing turned the colour of a piece of cantaloupe I had eaten the night before, and I gave up.

What follows is a week of plates made before the day got hot. Most are from before seven; one or two are from the long evening light. The whole feature was shot with the Leica M11 and a 35mm Summilux, on the same sensor settings every morning.

## A short note on the light

The Alentejo light at six is colder than you remember. By six-thirty it has started its long climb toward the warm hour, and by seven the shadows have already begun to harden. The window of the kind of light I was after — soft, directional, still pink at the edges — was about thirty minutes wide. I learned to be in position by ten to six.

## The street

The town does not move quickly in any season. In April, in the early morning, you can stand at the centre of a square for ten minutes and meet only the bread van and a woman watering a row of geraniums. The two of them are in plate No. 4. They were kind about being asked.

## The interior

I was given a key to the upstairs of a friend&rsquo;s parents&rsquo; house — a long room with three windows, a Singer sewing machine on the eastern wall, and a chair that had been in the same place for forty years. Most of the interior plates are from this room. The chair is plate No. 11.

## The drive home

The last two plates are from a stop on the road back, where I pulled over to read a book and look at a field of wheat the colour of warm sand. I made one frame and kept driving. It is not the strongest plate of the feature. It is the one I think about most.
