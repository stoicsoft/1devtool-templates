This site turns ten years old this spring. I started it in a kitchen in Porto in 2017, on a laptop that has since been recycled, using a static-site generator I have since stopped using. Ten years feels long enough to write down what it has actually cost.

## The money

In the first year I spent about €90 on a domain and €60 on hosting. Most years since have been cheaper. One year was more expensive because I tried a new hosting provider that charged for bandwidth I did not use. I went back to the old one.

Totalled across a decade, the site has cost me around €1,400, or about €140 a year. The largest line item is the domain renewal, which I always pay for five years in advance because I forget otherwise.

## The migrations

I have rebuilt the site three times. Each rebuild took a weekend. Each one deleted a feature that had seemed important three years earlier.

- The 2017 version had comments. The 2020 version did not.
- The 2020 version had an email list. The 2023 version still has one.
- The 2023 version had tag-cloud navigation. The 2026 version does not.

What remains, after the subtraction, is an essay page, a notes page, a links page, a bookshelf, an about page, and the RSS feed. That appears to be enough.

## The maintenance

I spend, on average, one Sunday morning a month on the site. Most of that time is writing. Some of it is quiet repair — updating a dependency, renewing a certificate, fixing a link that rotted. The maintenance is never urgent. It is never zero.

The site is written in Markdown, deployed from a git push. There is nothing on it that I could not, if pressed, rebuild inside an afternoon on a new laptop. That is the design principle I have cared about most.

## The things it has given back

Ten years of writing in one place has given me:

- A record of what I thought I thought, at various ages.
- A small number of friendships with people who found me through a post.
- A better sense of when I am being honest and when I am performing.
- An uncanny ability to remember what I was reading in September of 2022.

That is a strange set of things to get in exchange for €140 a year. I am not giving it up.
