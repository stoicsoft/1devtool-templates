The girl across from me on the metro was reading a paperback copy of Pessoa&rsquo;s *Livro do Desassossego* with a pen in her other hand, underlining passages. She was about seventeen, wearing an oversized school jumper, and did not look up once. The pen was a black Bic, uncapped.

I watched her for four stops. Every minute or so she would stop, hold the pen over the page, and either underline one or two words or not underline anything and turn the page.

It is possible, I think, that more people in this city are reading Pessoa on the metro than anywhere else in the world. It is also possible that this has always been true.
