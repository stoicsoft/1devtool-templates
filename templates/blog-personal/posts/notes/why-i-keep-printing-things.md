I print essays I have already read on screen. Sometimes the same essay twice. The printer lives in a cupboard in the hallway and, by the sound of it, is more expensive than the essays it prints.

I do this for three reasons.

- I read more carefully on paper. I catch sentences I would have skimmed.
- I can mark the paper without closing the thing I am reading.
- A printed essay does not compete with notifications.

None of this is a productivity argument. It is a pleasure argument. I like the way paper feels in my hand. I like the slight resistance of a pen dragging ink across it. I like that I can fold the corner of the page I want to come back to.

A folder in the hallway is slowly filling with essays I thought I was done with. I keep the folder because, every few months, I reach into it and find a paragraph I did not know I had been looking for.
